The Children’s LoveCastles Trust (CLT) story began in 1997 from a room with a view...

“From my apartment window, I would see children building castles out of the mud. Kids who should be spending their time in school were out accompanying their parents at the construction site. This scene which resonated with me would repeat itself day after day until I realized a universal truth – Children loved building castles and homes – a place of belonging, whether at the beach, a playground or here at a construction site. Children’s LoveCastles Trust (CLT) was born out of a passion to keep kids in school. To give them the education they deserve, so they can go build on their dreams and aspirations.”

BR

Bhagya Rangachar

Founder

There was a natural progression and evolution of our work into the education space while we were doing school mid-day meal programs. It gave us a window into the everyday struggle that the government schools were faced with: not being able to provide teachers for every grade, one-teacher-one-room schools, and teacher absenteeism at its worst. It would get much worse in rural areas to attract and retain qualified teachers.

1.2M

Teacher shortage in India today. Retaining qualified educators in rural government classrooms remains a persistent barrier to quality education.

Our idea was to have a hub to bring in the best of teachers to centralize pedagogy, map it to National curricula, customize it to every State, and leverage technology for its strength in replication and distribution. Personalized access and equity to quality education for every child at the local level has paid dividends.

Roadmap

Our Journey

A timeline of innovation, growth, and educational impact

1997

Trust Incorporated — Children's LoveCastles Trust is officially incorporated, starting with a room with a view.

1998

Mid-Day Meal Pioneered — Pioneered the school mid-day meal program for government school students in Karnataka.

2000

Srujana Centre Set Up — Setup the Srujana Learning Centre as a pilot for introducing ICT tools in government schools.

2002

Intel Partnership — Intel partnered with CLT to establish the only Intel Computer Club House—an after school program for under-served youth to explore technology.

2005

US Foundation Registration — CLT International Foundation Inc. is officially registered in Virginia, USA.

2006

e-Patashale Concept — Conceptualised the e-Patashale program as the first ever digital STEM program in regional languages, piloting in 100 schools.

2011

CISCO Alliance — Partnered with CISCO to establish a digital distance-learning program for government schools.

2012

Research Cell Established — Setup a dedicated research cell for pedagogy and technology to build a scalable and replicable model for e-Patashale.

2013

USAID FICCI Award — Won the USAID FICCI Millennium Alliance Award for low-cost technology innovation in education.

2014

Ecosystem Deployment — Partnered with CSR networks and the Department of Education to scale and build sustainable e-learning classrooms.

2015

Seven Changemakers — Identified as one of Seven Changemakers under the Dasra & USAID research on girl education impact in secondary schools.

2016

Digital Learning Box — Established the custom, SIM-enabled 'Digital Learning Box' in-house lab, enabling remote content updates and analytics.

2017

Hindi States Expansion — Extended the e-Patashale program to Hindi speaking states (UP, Bihar, Rajasthan) and started a pilot in Maharashtra.

2018

Jigi App Platform — Introduced the Jigi App Platform—custom mobile applications built specifically for teachers and learners.

2020

2nd Millennium Alliance Award — Received the 2nd Millennium Alliance Award for Early Grade Reading (EGR). Successfully launched the "Study from Home" campaign with JIGI-JIGI app downloads.

2021

Sabera Award & Expansion — Received the Sabera Award 2021. Launched teacher upskilling digital courses, reaching 180 schools and 36,000 students.

2022
Year Overview & Expansion:
  • Awards: Math power prize, GCPIT Award.
  • Programs: Girls' Educational Scholarships, Digital Literacy v1.5, Telugu Digital Literacy.
  • Partners: Menda Foundation (180 schools), Rotary Lakeside (40 schools), Aseem Infra (27 schools), Embassy Group.
2023

Program Expansion — Scaled the early grade reading courses and partnered with new rural schools to enhance learning outcomes.

2024

Digital Literacy Drive — Upgraded the Jigi platform with offline features and customized content modules for Hindi and Telugu speaking regions.

2025

Looking Ahead — Setting up digital hubs in underserved districts, targeting to reach over 50,000 students across multiple states.
